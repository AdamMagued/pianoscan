About
How PianoScan works.
🤖

AI Engine

Uses a deep learning model trained on thousands of scores to detect notes, rhythms, and symbols. It handles imperfect images well — ideal for phone photos, slightly blurry scans, or handwritten scores where lighting isn't perfect. Takes 2–3 minutes.

🎯

Precise Logic Engine

A classical rule-based system that traces staff lines, note heads, and musical symbols with high precision. Works best on crisp, high-contrast images — think sharp phone scans or printed sheet music photographed in good light. Much faster at 30–60 seconds.

🧬

Hybrid Engine

Runs both engines and merges their results measure by measure, picking the best interpretation for each section. Experimental — results vary, but when it works it catches things neither engine would alone. Takes 3–4 minutes.

🎹

What you get

A standard MIDI file — the universal format for digital music. Play it back here in the browser, or import it into any DAW (GarageBand, Logic, Ableton), digital piano, or notation software like MuseScore. One page at a time, no account needed.

TIP — Not sure which engine to pick? Start with Precise Logic if your image is clear and well-lit. Switch to AI if the result is missing notes or you're working with a lower quality photo. Use Hybrid if you want to experiment and don't mind the wait.
